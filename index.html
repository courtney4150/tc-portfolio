Proposal

Project 1

Proposal Abstract: This project seeks funding from the National Center of Women and Information Technology (NCWIT)’s AspireIT program to support a week-long computer science summer camp for middle school girls in this school district. This camp will provide a free introduction to basic programming concepts and languages to female students where they otherwise don’t have the opportunity to learn about this field. Students will come out of this program with a strong understanding of basic computing concepts and connections to female role models and classmates in the technology field. If funded, our team will immediately begin organizing the camp for this coming summer.

Safety Document

Project 2

In making my safety document, I focused on using white space and legible font to emphasize the five safety points. This draws the viewer’s attention directly to the important information. I use repetition to keep the formatting of each icon and safety point consistent. This document is intended to be used in a specific workshop environment and be easy to read so that students and mentors can quickly read the safety reminders. I wanted the document to be simple yet effective: the use of white space and consistent fonts and colors makes the document look clean. The contrast of white and navy blue helps make the font easy to read.

Infographic

Project 3

In making my infographic, I focused on using white space and legible font to emphasize the data in a clear and concise manner. The consistent font and colors draw the viewer’s attention directly down the document. The word “ballet” and the ballet shoes are the largest components of the infographic. The title quickly grabs the viewer’s attention and the shoes provide immediate context on the topic of the document. This infographic is intended to provide the basic information about ballet as a profession, giving statistics about the average wage, the number of people in the workforce, the average workforce age, and the breakdown of the workforce by gender.

Training Module

Project 4

The sustainable development training manual design features 5 sections, each containing 2-3 subsections with additional information. My team decided to show a navigation panel to the left at all times so users can choose to view the modules in order or jump around. We opted not to use a quiz format to put the emphasis on learning something new rather than passing a quiz. The informational approach has some structure but still allows the user to choose what specific topics they want to learn more about. The manual provides a resource for fellow University of Pittsburgh students to learn more about sustainability and sustainable development in Pittsburgh. Making all of the resources easily accessible in the navigation panel helps achieve our goal of raising awareness of sustainability.

Usability Study Report

Project 5

Overall, my 8-question usability survey found that respondents reported having a positive experience with the product. All of the respondents were comfortable with the navigation and flow of the manual and they thought the design of the manual was consistent. No aspects of the manual made any respondent feel confused or upset. The navigation was mostly intuitive to users, but presented some issues when scrolling or on a mobile device. The biggest piece of feedback was improving the scrolling and mobile experience.

Oral Presentation

Project 6

Using the assertion-evidence presentation technique, I shared how artificial intelligence technology can produce racially biased outcomes. I looked at three different examples: facial recognition technology, hiring practices, and a conversational chat-bot. I also discussed ways to combat these biases: diverse teams, better data, and auditing.
